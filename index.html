Lost Worlds: Ancient Civilizations

Delve into the mysteries of ancient civilizations with the timeline titled "Lost Worlds: Ancient Civilizations." This captivating journey through time unveils pivotal events that shaped the course of humanity, from the monumental constructions to the enigmatic disappearances.

  • Building of the Great Pyramid of Giza (c. 2580–2560 BCE)

    Witness the awe-inspiring endeavor of the ancient Egyptians as they constructed one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, the Great Pyramid of Giza. Standing tall for millennia, it remains a testament to their architectural prowess and ingenuity.

  • Disappearance of the Indus Valley Civilization (c. 1900 BCE)

    Explore the enigmatic vanishing act of the Indus Valley Civilization, one of the world's oldest urban societies. Despite their advanced urban planning and sophisticated culture, the reasons behind their sudden decline continue to puzzle historians.

  • Construction of Stonehenge (c. 3000–2000 BCE)

    Marvel at the mysterious stone circle of Stonehenge, erected by ancient Britons over thousands of years. Its purpose and significance remain shrouded in mystery, sparking speculation and intrigue for centuries.

  • Collapse of the Mayan Civilization (c. 800–1000 CE)

    Witness the fall of the mighty Mayan Civilization, known for its remarkable achievements in astronomy, mathematics, and architecture. Yet, despite their advancements, internal strife and environmental challenges led to their eventual collapse, leaving behind a legacy of wonder and fascination.

Embark on a captivating journey through time, unraveling the secrets of these ancient civilizations and their enduring legacies that continue to intrigue and inspire us today.

2580–2560 BCE

Building of the Great Pyramid of Giza

Standing at 481ft tall, the Great Pyramid of Giza was once the final resting place of King Khufu and a temple to the sun god Re. It is one of the most remarkable architectural achievements of the Ancient Egyptians.

1900 BCE

Disappearance of the Indus Valley Civilization

The Indus Valley Civilization began in the Bronze Age and populated from modern-day northeast Afghanistan to Pakistan and northwest India. The civilization began to dissapear around 1900 BCE which experts believe was due to climate change caused by the Saraswati River drying.

3000–2000 BCE

Construction of Stonehenge

Stonehenge was constructed during 3000–2000 BCE. Found on England’s Salisbury Plain in Wiltshire, Stonehenge is a man-made circle of standing stones.

800–1000 CE

Collapse of the Mayan Civilization

The Mayan Civilization around A.D. 900 had finally established almost all of the major cities in what was then the heart of Maya civilization. The civilization diminished due to overpopulation, environmental degradation, warfare, shifting trade routes and extended drought. Today many famous Mayan Temples remain one of which is known as Chichen Itza.

Unsolved Cases: Mysteries Through the Ages

Embark on a journey through the shadows of history with the timeline titled "Mysteries Through the Ages." This collection of enigmatic events offers a glimpse into some of the most perplexing unsolved cases that have mystified generations.

  • The Roanoke Colony Disappearance (1590)

    Step into the intrigue surrounding the mysterious disappearance of the Roanoke Colony, where an entire settlement in the New World vanished without a trace. The cryptic message "Croatoan" left behind only deepens the mystery of their fate.

  • The Lost Colony of Greenland (c. 1400s)

    Explore the chilling tale of the Lost Colony of Greenland, where Norse settlers established a thriving community before inexplicably vanishing from history. The haunting silence that followed leaves historians puzzled to this day.

  • The Voynich Manuscript Discovery (c. 1400–1500s)

    Encounter the enigmatic Voynich Manuscript, an ancient text filled with cryptic illustrations and an unknown script that has defied decipherment for centuries. Its origins and purpose remain shrouded in secrecy, inviting speculation and debate.

  • The Tamam Shud Case (1948)

    Unravel the baffling mystery of the Tamam Shud Case, where an unidentified man was found dead on an Australian beach with a cryptic code hidden in his pocket. Despite extensive investigations, the true identity of the man and the meaning behind the code remain elusive.

Enter the realm of the unknown as you explore these unsolved cases, where each mystery serves as a testament to the enduring fascination with the inexplicable and the insatiable human quest for answers.

1590

The Roanoke Colony Disappearance

The Roanoke Island settlers arrived in 1587 and vanished in 1590, leaving behind "Croatoan" on a fort's gatepost and "Cro" on a tree. Their disappearance coincided with a severe drought, affecting nearby colonies.

1400s

The Lost Colony of Greenland

The settlers' disappearance from Greenland in 1410 remains a perplexing mystery. The last recorded voyage from Greenland in 1410 marks the end of Norse settlements. The traditional belief suggests their downfall stemmed from adhering to European farming practices unsuitable for Greenland's harsh environment.

1400–1500s

The Voynich Manuscript Discovery

The Voynich manuscript, an illustrated codex, features an unknown script called 'Voynichese.' Bowern refutes claims of gibberish, suggesting it's encoded in a natural coded or constructed language, deepening the intrigue surrounding this mysterious text.

1948

The Tamam Shud Case

In 1948, an unknown man was discovered deceased on Somerton Beach in Australia. With no identification, only a torn scrap with the words "Tamam Shud" ("ended" in Persian) found in his pocket, the mystery deepens.

Historical Enigmas: Riddles of History

Unlock the secrets of the past with the captivating timeline titled "Riddles of History." Delve into the intriguing enigmas that have puzzled historians and scientists for generations, offering glimpses into the mysteries that continue to fascinate us to this day.

  • The Antikythera Mechanism (c. 100 BCE)

    Journey back to ancient Greece and marvel at the Antikythera Mechanism, a remarkable ancient device discovered in the depths of the Mediterranean Sea. Its intricate gears and mechanisms hint at an advanced understanding of astronomy and technology far ahead of its time.

  • The Shroud of Turin's Origins (c. 14th century)

    Contemplate the mysterious origins of the Shroud of Turin, a linen cloth bearing the faint image of a man believed by some to be Jesus Christ. Its authenticity and the nature of the image it bears continue to spark debate and intrigue among scholars and believers alike.

  • The Dancing Plague of 1518

    Witness the bizarre phenomenon of the Dancing Plague of 1518, where hundreds of people in Strasbourg, France, inexplicably danced uncontrollably for days on end. The cause of this mass hysteria remains a subject of speculation, offering a haunting glimpse into the power of the human mind.

  • The Tunguska Event (1908)

    Explore the mysteries of the Tunguska Event, a massive explosion that occurred over the remote Siberian wilderness in 1908. The origin of this cataclysmic event, whether it was a meteorite impact or some other phenomenon, continues to elude scientists, leaving behind a legacy of speculation and wonder.

Embark on a journey through time and unravel the enigmas of history, where each event serves as a tantalizing puzzle waiting to be solved, offering insights into the mysteries of our shared past.

100BCE

The Antikythera Mechanism

Recovered from a shipwreck off Antikythera in 1901, the Antikythera Mechanism, dating to around 100 BCE, is a remarkable ancient Greek computer. It accurately forecasted astronomical positions and eclipses, serving calendar and astrological needs.

14th century

The Shroud of Turin's Origins

The Shroud of Turin, revered as Jesus's burial cloth bearing his image, underwent radio-carbon testing placing its origins in the 14th century with 95% certainty. Despite this, it remains a cherished holy relic of faith and intrigue.

1518

The Dancing Plague of 1518

In 1518, Strasbourg was consumed by the inexplicable Dancing Plague, where hundreds danced uncontrollably for weeks. Originating in Strasbourg, then part of the Holy Roman Empire, the phenomenon ceased as mysteriously as it began after two months.

1908

The Tunguska Event

The Tunguska event, a colossal explosion in 1908, devastated over 2,000 square km of Siberian forest. While some attribute it to a meteor impact due to noctilucent clouds, others suggest an asteroid impact, sparking ongoing debate among scientists.
